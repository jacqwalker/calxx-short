Less is always more

2 May 2018

Photo by Álvaro Serrano on Unsplash

Ernest Hemingway once bet a friend that he could tell a story in six words.

The result: "For sale: baby shoes, never worn."

Why does the corporate world encourage more? 11-hour work days. 60 deck slide packs. Ten-page reports. Management packs filled with graphs and analysis, where most the content is not even relevant or read.

Is this to prove how busy we are? Or does it show lazy thinking and indiscriminate action to quote Tim Ferris?

Let’s stop the laziness epidemic and be more like Ernest Hemingway.

Less is always more.
